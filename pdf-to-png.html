PDF vs PNG: Understanding the Formats

PDF (Portable Document Format) was created by Adobe in 1993 and has become the universal standard for document exchange. PDFs preserve formatting across all devices and operating systems, making them ideal for sharing documents that need to appear exactly as intended. However, PDFs can be difficult to edit, embed in websites, or use in design software.

PNG (Portable Network Graphics) is a raster graphics format developed as an improved, patent-free alternative to GIF. PNG offers lossless compression, meaning no quality is lost during compression, and supports full alpha transparency. PNG images are perfect for web use, digital design, and situations where you need to preserve every detail of your content.

Converting between these formats gives you the best of both worlds: the reliable structure of PDF for distribution, and the versatile image format of PNG for editing, embedding, and displaying across all platforms.
